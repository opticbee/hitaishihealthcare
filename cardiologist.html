Heart Health Prevention Tips

Healthy Diet

Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins and healthy fats. Limit salt, sugar and processed foods.

Regular Exercise

Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly. Even walking 30 minutes daily can significantly improve heart health.

Stress Management

Chronic stress damages your heart. Practice meditation, deep breathing, yoga or other relaxation techniques.

Quality Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours nightly. Poor sleep is linked to higher risk of heart disease and stroke.

No Smoking

Smoking damages blood vessels and greatly increases cardiovascular risks. We offer smoking cessation programs.

Regular Checkups

Monitor blood pressure, cholesterol and other risk factors. Early detection saves lives.

Alcohol Moderation

Limit alcohol to 1 drink/day for women, 2 for men. Excessive drinking can lead to high blood pressure and heart failure.

Weight Management

Maintain a healthy BMI. Even 5-10% weight loss can significantly improve blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Cardiac Emergency Warning

Call emergency services immediately for chest pain, shortness of breath, or other heart attack symptoms.

Heart Attack Signs:

  • ✓ Chest discomfort
  • ✓ Arm/jaw pain
  • ✓ Shortness of breath
  • ✓ Cold sweat
  • ✓ Nausea
  • ✓ Dizziness

Frequently Asked Questions

You should consider seeing a cardiologist if you experience chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness, fainting spells, irregular heartbeats, or if you have risk factors like high blood pressure, diabetes, family history of heart disease, or if you're a smoker. Annual checkups are recommended for those over 40 with risk factors.

Cardiologists are physicians who diagnose and treat heart conditions medically or with minimally invasive procedures. Cardiac surgeons perform open-heart surgeries and complex surgical interventions. Our cardiologists work closely with top cardiac surgeons when surgical options are needed.

Adults 20+ should have cholesterol checked every 4-6 years if risk is low. Those with risk factors (family history, high blood pressure, diabetes, smoking) may need testing annually. After 40, more frequent testing is typically recommended. Your cardiologist will create a personalized screening plan based on your risk profile.

Most palpitations are harmless, caused by stress, caffeine, or minor arrhythmias. However, if accompanied by chest pain, fainting, or severe shortness of breath, seek immediate care. Persistent or frequent palpitations should be evaluated by a cardiologist to rule out serious conditions.

Common signs include chest pressure/pain (may radiate to arm, jaw, or back), shortness of breath, cold sweat, nausea, lightheadedness. Women may experience atypical symptoms like fatigue, indigestion, or back pain. If you suspect a heart attack, call emergency services immediately - every minute counts.
