Skin Health Tips

Daily Protection

Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen every morning, even on cloudy days. Reapply every 2 hours when outdoors.

Gentle Cleansing

Use lukewarm water and mild, fragrance-free cleansers. Avoid harsh scrubbing that can damage your skin barrier.

Hydration

Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily and use moisturizers appropriate for your skin type immediately after bathing.

Self-Exams

Perform monthly skin checks using mirrors to inspect all areas. Look for new or changing moles or spots.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I see a dermatologist?

Most adults should have an annual skin check for cancer screening. Those with a history of skin cancer may need more frequent visits (every 3-6 months). For cosmetic or medical skin conditions, your dermatologist will recommend an appropriate schedule based on your treatment plan.

What's the difference between medical and cosmetic dermatology?

Medical dermatology focuses on diagnosing and treating skin diseases (acne, eczema, skin cancer, etc.). Cosmetic dermatology enhances appearance through procedures like Botox, fillers, lasers, and chemical peels. Many dermatologists practice both, as healthy skin is the foundation of beauty.

Are cosmetic procedures covered by insurance?

Most cosmetic procedures are considered elective and not covered by insurance. However, some treatments for medically necessary conditions (like laser for rosacea or removal of precancerous lesions) may be covered. Our staff can help verify your benefits for specific services.

How do I prepare for my first dermatology appointment?

Come with clean skin (no makeup or nail polish), a list of current medications/skincare products, and photos if you're tracking changes in a mole or rash. For medical concerns, note when symptoms began and what makes them better/worse. For cosmetic consultations, bring inspiration photos.
