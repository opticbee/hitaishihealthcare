Neurological Emergencies

If you or someone you know is experiencing any of the following symptoms, seek emergency medical attention immediately:

  • Sudden severe headache (worst headache of your life)
  • Sudden weakness or numbness, especially on one side
  • Difficulty speaking or understanding speech
  • Loss of consciousness or seizure
  • Sudden vision changes

Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. Time is critical for stroke and other neurological emergencies.

Neurology FAQs

You should consider seeing a neurologist if you experience persistent symptoms like: severe headaches, dizziness, numbness or tingling, movement problems, memory issues, seizures, or changes in vision/speech that aren't explained by other conditions. Your primary care physician may also refer you if they suspect a neurological issue.

For your first visit, please bring: your insurance card, photo ID, list of current medications, any relevant medical records or test results, a list of your symptoms with timeline, and questions you want to ask. If possible, bring a family member or friend to help remember information.

Some neurological conditions have genetic components, including Alzheimer's disease, Huntington's disease, certain forms of epilepsy, and some neuromuscular disorders. However, most neurological conditions result from a combination of genetic and environmental factors. Our genetic counseling services can help assess your risk.

Neurologists specialize in diagnosing and treating neurological conditions with medications and non-surgical therapies. Neurosurgeons perform surgical procedures on the brain, spine, and nerves. Our neurologists work closely with neurosurgeons when surgical intervention may be beneficial, and can provide referrals if needed.
